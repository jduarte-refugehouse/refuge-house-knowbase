## OVERVIEW

Refuge House, Inc. has selected **Trust-Based Relational Intervention (TBRI®)** as our evidence-informed treatment model for the T3C Basic Foster Family Home Support Services Package and all applied Add-On Services. TBRI is a holistic, research-based intervention designed specifically for children who have experienced relational trauma, making it ideally suited for children in foster care.

## TBRI FRAMEWORK

TBRI addresses the comprehensive needs of traumatized children through three integrated principles:

### 1\. CONNECTING PRINCIPLES

- Building trust through attunement and engagement  
- Providing felt safety in all interactions  
- Supporting healthy attachment development  
- Creating healing relationships as the foundation for change

### 2\. EMPOWERING PRINCIPLES

- Meeting physical and sensory needs  
- Establishing predictable routines and transitions  
- Supporting physiological regulation  
- Creating environments that promote healing

### 3\. CORRECTING PRINCIPLES

- Using proactive teaching strategies  
- Implementing the IDEAL Response® framework  
- Maintaining connection while addressing behaviors  
- Teaching self-regulation and life skills

## EVIDENCE BASE

TBRI's selection is supported by robust research and recognition:

- **California Evidence-Based Clearinghouse (CEBC) Rating:** TBRI® has received a Scientific Rating of **3 \- Promising Research Evidence**  
- **Recognized by SAMHSA** as meeting all six principles of trauma-informed care  
- **One of only four approaches globally** meeting essential trauma-informed system elements  
- **TCU research demonstrates:**  
  - 39% reduction in serious incidents  
  - 60% reduction in physical restraints  
  - Significant improvements in attachment behaviors  
  - Measurable decrease in caregiver stress  
  - Enhanced placement stability

## IMPLEMENTATION APPROACH

Our TBRI implementation is comprehensive and systematic:

### Organizational Integration

- **Leadership Commitment:** Board-approved as foundational treatment model  
- **Policy Integration:** TBRI principles embedded in all policies and procedures  
- **Staff Structure:** Dedicated roles for TBRI implementation and fidelity monitoring  
- **Resource Allocation:** Budget committed for training, materials, and ongoing support

### Training and Development

- **Staff Training:** All staff complete TBRI practitioner training with annual refreshers  
- **Foster Parent Education:** 35+ hours of pre-service TBRI training with ongoing support  
- **Child/Youth Education:** Age-appropriate materials and activities for TBRI awareness  
- **Continuous Learning:** Monthly consultation and quarterly skill-building sessions

### Quality Assurance

- **Fidelity Monitoring:** Quarterly assessments using TBRI implementation checklists  
- **Outcome Tracking:** CANS 3.0 scores, placement stability, and behavioral indicators  
- **Continuous Improvement:** Data-driven adjustments through CQI process  
- **Regular Review:** Annual comprehensive evaluation of TBRI effectiveness
