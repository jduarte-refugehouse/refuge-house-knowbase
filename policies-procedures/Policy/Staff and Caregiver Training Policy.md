## PURPOSE

To establish comprehensive training requirements for all personnel involved in T3C service delivery, ensuring consistent competency across staff, foster family caregivers, and contracted treatment team members. This policy centralizes training standards to support quality service delivery, regulatory compliance, and effective implementation of Refuge House's Evidence-informed Treatment Model (TBRI®).

---

## POLICY STATEMENT

Refuge House is committed to ensuring all individuals involved in caring for children possess the knowledge, skills, and competencies necessary for trauma-informed, effective service delivery. Training is not merely a compliance requirement but a foundational element of quality care that directly impacts child outcomes.

All training at Refuge House integrates Trust-Based Relational Intervention® (TBRI®) principles, ensuring a consistent therapeutic approach across all caregiving contexts. Training requirements are tailored to the specific responsibilities and populations served by each role and Service Package.

---

## SCOPE

This policy applies to:

**Agency Staff:**
- Executive leadership (Executive Director, Program Directors, Treatment Directors)
- Direct service staff (Case Managers, Intake/Placement Specialists)
- Clinical staff (Licensed Therapists employed by agency)
- Support staff (Administrative, Quality Assurance, Training Coordinators)

**Foster Family Home Caregivers:**
- Prospective foster parents (pre-service)
- Verified foster parents (ongoing/annual)
- Foster home staff employed by foster parents
- Respite caregivers

**Contracted Treatment Team Members:**
- Licensed Therapists (contracted through STAR Health network)
- Behavioral Support Specialists/Mentors
- Registered Nurses and nurse consultants
- Psychiatrists and psychiatric consultants
- Other specialists providing services to children in care

**Kinship Caregivers:**
- Relative caregivers
- Fictive kin caregivers

## PROVISIONS

### 1. TRAINING PHILOSOPHY

Refuge House training is grounded in the following principles:

**1.1 TBRI® Integration**

All training incorporates Trust-Based Relational Intervention® principles:
- **Connecting Principles**: Building trust and attachment through relationship
- **Empowering Principles**: Meeting physical and environmental needs
- **Correcting Principles**: Using proactive and responsive strategies

**1.2 Trauma-Informed Approach**

Training delivery and content acknowledge:
- Impact of trauma on child development and behavior
- Secondary traumatic stress for caregivers and staff
- Need for self-care and support systems
- Strengths-based, non-punitive approaches

**1.3 Competency-Based Learning**

Training focuses on demonstrated competency, not merely attendance:
- Knowledge acquisition verified through assessment
- Skills demonstrated through practice and observation
- Ongoing coaching and support to maintain competency

### 2. STAFF TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

**2.1 Pre-Employment/Orientation Training**

All new staff must complete within **30 days of hire**:

| Training Component | Hours | Requirement Source |
|-------------------|-------|-------------------|
| Agency Orientation | 2 | Refuge House Policy |
| T3C System Overview | 2 | T3C Blueprint |
| TBRI® Foundations | 4 | T3C Blueprint (Treatment Model) |
| Human Trafficking Prevention | 2-3 | T3C Blueprint p.18-19 |
| Policies and Procedures Review | 2 | TAC §749.931 |
| Documentation and Radius Training | 2 | Refuge House Policy |
| **TOTAL MINIMUM** | **14-15** | |

**2.2 Position-Specific Training**

**Executive Leadership** (Program Director, Treatment Director):
- T3C Service Package requirements and compliance (4 hours)
- Quality assurance and oversight responsibilities (2 hours)
- Clinical supervision (Treatment Director) (4 hours)

**Case Managers**:
- Service planning and CANS 3.0 administration (8 hours)
- STAR Health coordination (4 hours)
- Family engagement and permanency planning (4 hours)
- Package-specific training for assigned caseloads (varies)

**Clinical Staff**:
- TBRI® clinical application (8 hours)
- T3C clinical requirements by Service Package (varies)
- Crisis intervention and safety planning (4 hours)

**2.3 Annual Staff Training**

All staff must complete **20 hours minimum** annually, including:
- TBRI® refresher/advanced application (4 hours)
- Policy and regulatory updates (2 hours)
- Human Trafficking Prevention refresher (as required)
- Position-specific continuing education (varies)
- Package-specific updates for assigned populations (varies)

### 3. FOSTER FAMILY CAREGIVER TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

**3.1 Pre-Service Training**

Prospective foster parents must complete **35 hours minimum** before verification (TAC §749.863, §749.885):

| Training Component | Hours | Requirement Source |
|-------------------|-------|-------------------|
| RH 101 Orientation | 2 | Refuge House Policy |
| Pre-Service Curriculum | 12 | TAC §749.863, §749.885 |
| SAMA (Behavioral Management) | 16 | Refuge House Policy |
| CPR/First Aid | 2 | TAC §749.911(1) |
| Psychotropic Medications | 2 | TAC §749.911 |
| Transportation Safety | 2 | Refuge House Policy |
| Medical Consent | 1 | DFPS Requirement |
| **TOTAL** | **37** | |

**Additional Pre-Service Requirements:**
- TBRI® Foundations integrated throughout curriculum
- Human Trafficking Prevention (2-3 hours) per T3C Blueprint
- Background checks and home study completion per FC-17

**3.2 T3C Transition Training (Current Foster Parents)**

Existing verified foster parents transitioning to T3C credentialing must complete **16 hours**:

| Module | Hours | Content |
|--------|-------|---------|
| T3C Orientation & Vision | 2 | T3C transformation; what changes |
| TBRI® as Treatment Model | 4 | TBRI principles with T3C application |
| Human Trafficking Prevention | 2 | DFPS curriculum (if not current) |
| Service Planning & Documentation | 4 | Foster parent participation; outcome tracking |
| Enhanced Family Connections | 2 | Family engagement expectations |
| Add-On Services Integration | 2 | Kinship, Transition, Pregnant/Parenting overview |

**3.3 Annual Caregiver Training**

Verified foster parents must complete **30 hours minimum** annually (TAC §749.931):

| Training Category | Hours | Requirement Source |
|------------------|-------|-------------------|
| TBRI® Application/Refresher | 4 | T3C Blueprint |
| Behavior Management (SAMA refresher) | 8 | Refuge House Policy |
| Trauma-Informed Care | 2 | TAC §749.911 |
| Cultural Competency | 2 | TAC §749.911 |
| Normalcy/Prudent Parenting | 2 | TAC §749.911(13) |
| Child Development | 2 | TAC §749.911(2) |
| CPR/First Aid (maintain certification) | 2 | TAC §749.911(1) |
| Electives/Package-Specific | 8 | Various |
| **TOTAL** | **30** | |

### 5. CONTRACTED TREATMENT TEAM MEMBER REQUIREMENTS

**5.1 Verification of Qualifications**

Before engaging contracted treatment team members, Refuge House verifies:
- Current professional licensure/certification
- Required background checks per TAC Chapter 749
- Liability insurance coverage
- Experience with child welfare population

**5.2 Orientation Requirements**

All contracted treatment team members must receive orientation including:

| Component | Method | Timeline |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Refuge House mission and values | Written materials | Before service delivery |
| TBRI® Treatment Model overview | Written materials or brief training | Before service delivery |
| T3C Service Package requirements | Written materials | Before service delivery |
| Documentation and communication expectations | Meeting with Case Manager | Before service delivery |
| Reporting requirements | Written materials | Before service delivery |

**5.3 Provider-Specific Requirements**

**Licensed Therapists (Contracted):**
- Must be credentialed with STAR Health managed care organization
- Encouraged to complete TBRI® training
- Must participate in treatment team coordination
- Annual verification of licensure and credentials

**Behavioral Support Specialists/Mentors:**
- Must meet T3C Blueprint qualifications
- TBRI® Foundations training (4 hours minimum)
- Package-specific training for populations served
- Annual competency verification

**Nursing Staff (Contracted):**
- Current RN or LVN licensure
- Orientation to T3C health coordination requirements
- TBRI® awareness training
- Trauma-informed care awareness

**5.4 Ongoing Coordination**

Contracted treatment team members receive:
- Regular communication from Case Manager regarding child progress
- Invitation to Service Planning Team meetings
- Updates on Treatment Model expectations
- Access to consultation with Treatment Director as needed

### 6. KINSHIP CAREGIVER TRAINING

**6.1 Modified Training Approach**

Recognizing kinship caregivers' existing relationships with children, training is adapted while maintaining safety standards:

| Training Component | Hours | Timeline |
|-------------------|-------|----------|
| Kinship-Specific Orientation | 2 | Within 14 days of placement |
| Safety and Emergency Procedures | 2 | Within 14 days of placement |
| Trauma-Informed Parenting Basics | 4 | Within 30 days |
| TBRI® Foundations (modified) | 4 | Within 60 days |
| Human Trafficking Prevention | 2 | Within 90 days |
| CPR/First Aid | 2 | Within 90 days |
| **TOTAL PRE-VERIFICATION** | **16** | |

**6.2 Ongoing Kinship Training**

- Annual training requirements per TAC §749.931 (30 hours)
- Additional support and coaching as identified
- Access to all foster parent training opportunities

### 7. HUMAN TRAFFICKING PREVENTION TRAINING

**Cross-Reference:** HT-POL-001 Universal Human Trafficking Prevention Training Policy

**7.1 Universal Requirement**

ALL staff, caregivers, and contracted treatment team members must complete Human Trafficking Prevention Training:

| Audience | Initial Training | Refresher |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Staff | Within 30 days of hire | Every 2 years |
| Foster Caregivers | Pre-service | Every 2 years |
| Contracted Providers | Before service delivery | Every 2 years |
| Kinship Caregivers | Within 90 days | Every 2 years |

**7.2 Training Delivery**

Refuge House maintains certified trainers per DFPS Train-the-Trainer model. See HT-POL-001 and HT-PROC-001 for complete requirements.

---

### 8. TRAINING DOCUMENTATION AND COMPLIANCE

**8.1 Documentation Requirements**

All training must be documented including:
- Training title and content
- Date(s) of completion
- Hours credited
- Trainer/provider information
- Certificate of completion (where applicable)
- Competency assessment results (where applicable)

**8.2 Record Maintenance**

Training records maintained in:
- **Staff**: Personnel files and Learning Management System (LMS)
- **Caregivers**: Licensing files in Radius and LMS
- **Contracted Providers**: Provider files with annual verification

**8.3 Compliance Monitoring**

Training Coordinator monitors:
- Pre-service completion before placement approval
- Annual training compliance by anniversary date
- Package-specific training before serving specialized populations
- Expiring certifications (CPR, etc.)

**8.4 Non-Compliance Response**

- 30-day warning for approaching non-compliance
- Placement hold for caregivers not meeting requirements
- Performance management for staff non-compliance
- Contract review for non-compliant providers

### 9. QUALITY ASSURANCE

**9.1 Training Effectiveness**

Training effectiveness assessed through:
- Post-training knowledge assessments
- Competency demonstrations

**9.2 Continuous Improvement**

Training program improvements informed by:
- Participant evaluations

## DEFINITIONS

- **Caregiver**: Foster parent or other adult providing direct care to children in Refuge House foster homes
- **Contracted Treatment Team Member**: Licensed professional providing services to children in care through contract arrangement rather than direct employment
- **Competency**: Demonstrated ability to apply knowledge and skills in practice
- **Pre-Service Training**: Training completed before initial foster home verification or employment start
- **Annual Training**: Training completed within each 12-month period following initial training
- **Package-Specific Training**: Additional training required for personnel serving children in specialized T3C Service Packages
- **TBRI®**: Trust-Based Relational Intervention® - Refuge House's evidence-informed treatment model
- **MAT**: Medication-Assisted Treatment for substance use disorders
