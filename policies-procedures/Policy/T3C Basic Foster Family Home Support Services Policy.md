## PURPOSE:

To establish Refuge House's commitment to providing high-quality, trauma-informed foster care services under the Texas Child-Centered Care (T3C) Basic Foster Family Home Support Services Package, ensuring comprehensive assessment, individualized service planning, family connections, and successful transitions for children in care while meeting all regulatory requirements including Foster Family Home credentialing, CANS 3.0 assessments, comprehensive support services, and aftercare services.

## POLICY:

Refuge House provides T3C Basic Foster Family Home Support Services through credentialed, trauma-informed foster homes that offer comprehensive support using Trust-Based Relational Intervention® (TBRI®) as our Evidence-informed Treatment Model. Our approach ensures that children receive individualized, strength-based care that promotes safety, permanency, and well-being while maintaining robust documentation and service coordination across all involved systems.

Believing a home is in the heart of every child, Refuge House strives to recruit, support, and retain a diverse group of foster parents to provide quality care for the vast needs of children awaiting placement. Through comprehensive credentialing, ongoing support, and evidence-based practices, we ensure our Foster Family Homes can effectively serve children with trauma histories and complex needs.

**Anticipated Length of Service:** 12-18 months typical duration, customized based on individual needs and Continued Stay Guidelines as outlined in the T3C Blueprint.

## CORE REQUIREMENTS:

### 1\. Foster Family Home Credentialing and Assessment Process

Refuge House maintains a comprehensive process to assess and credential Foster Family Homes for the T3C Basic Foster Family Home Support Services Package. This process goes beyond standard DFPS licensing to evaluate specialized competencies required for trauma-informed care delivery.

**Initial Credentialing Requirements:**

- All Foster Family Homes must be assessed and credentialed for the T3C Basic Foster Family Home Support Services Package  
- Credentialing is valid for 4 years per T3C Blueprint requirements  
- Foster homes must maintain separate credentials for base package and each add-on service  
- Credentialing includes assessment of TBRI® training completion, trauma-informed care experience, physical environment safety assessment, and support system evaluation

**Assessment Process:** Building on our existing recruitment and retention processes, credentialing assessment includes initial home study verification, TBRI® training completion and competency demonstration, experience with trauma-informed care approaches, physical environment therapeutic appropriateness, support system evaluation and crisis response capacity, and understanding of T3C service delivery requirements.

**Re-Credentialing Process:**

- Annual compliance review and capacity utilization evaluation per T3C Blueprint requirements  
- Child outcome data review and quality improvement participation  
- Ongoing training and competency maintenance verification

### 2\. CANS 3.0 Assessment Requirements

Youth over the age of 3 will receive a CANS 3.0 Assessment annually as required by the T3C Blueprint. These comprehensive assessments guide service planning and service adjustments throughout the child's placement, ensuring individualized care that addresses evolving needs and strengths.

**Assessment Implementation:**

- Annual CANS 3.0 assessments conducted by licensed clinical staff  
- Results directly inform service planning and service plan modifications  
- Assessment findings integrated into individual service plans within required timeframes  
- Progress monitoring and outcome tracking based on CANS 3.0 domains

**Service Plan Integration:**

- Adjustment of services based on subsequent CANS 3.0 Assessment(s) and Service Plan  
- Program Director review to ensure alignment with youth's custom strengths, needs, and permanency plan  
- Written confirmation that youth continues to meet criteria and is benefitting from the program  
- Documentation in CPA's case record with copy to SSCC/DFPS caseworker within 15 business days of review

### 3\. Foster Family Support Requirements

Foster Family Home Caregivers offer logistical support, coordination, and documentation/record keeping of services in accordance with court orders and the Service Plan. This comprehensive support approach ensures seamless service delivery and maintains the therapeutic milieu necessary for child healing and growth.

**Support Services:**

- Transportation coordination and provision for therapeutic appointments  
- Documentation of service delivery and child progress  
- Coordination with service providers per court orders and service plans  
- Implementation of therapeutic recommendations in daily caregiving  
- Crisis response and stabilization using TBRI® principles

**24/7 Support Availability:**

- Agency staff available around the clock for crisis consultation  
- On-call support for emergency situations and urgent needs  
- Immediate response protocols for placement stability concerns  
- Regular consultation and coaching for foster families

### 4\. Service Denial Response

Refuge House will ensure delivery of, and cover the cost of needed services and related supports that are denied by insurance or other funding sources. This commitment ensures that children receive necessary services without interruption, regardless of external barriers to service access.

**Service Coverage:**

- Assessment of denied services and alternative funding identification  
- Direct agency coverage for essential therapeutic and support services  
- Coordination with alternative providers when primary sources are unavailable  
- Documentation and reporting of service denials and agency responses

### 5\. Therapy Services

Children in non-specialized behavioral health packages will receive regular and frequent individual and family therapy as required by the T3C Blueprint. This therapeutic support is integrated with daily care provided by Foster Family Home Caregivers to ensure comprehensive trauma-informed treatment.

**Therapeutic Service Delivery:**

- Individual therapy sessions addressing trauma and behavioral health needs  
- Family therapy including Foster Family Home Caregivers to support placement stability  
- Integration of therapeutic goals with daily caregiving practices  
- Coordination between therapists and foster families for consistent approach

### 6\. Aftercare Services

Comprehensive aftercare services are provided for 6 months following discharge from placement, ensuring continued support during transition and reducing risk of placement disruption or re-entry into care.

**Months 1-3: Intensive Support**

- Weekly contact through calls or meetings  
- Crisis intervention and resource connection  
- Transition support and stability monitoring

**Months 4-6: Continued Support**

- Twice monthly follow-up calls and/or meetings with flexible scheduling to accommodate youth/family availability  
- Ongoing resource referrals and case management support  
- Document ALL contacts or attempted contacts, referrals, and other case management support  
- Submit comprehensive documentation to SSCC/DFPS caseworker at the end of each month

## ADD-ON SERVICE REQUIREMENTS:

### 7\. ☑ Transition Support Services for Youth & Young Adults

For youth receiving Transition Support Services Add-On, Refuge House provides enhanced support for independence preparation through staff with expertise in PAL Program coordination and understanding of state and federal benefits for youth in and after foster care.

**Service Requirements:**

- Staff and Foster Family Home Caregivers must have expertise in PAL Program  
- Understanding of state and federal benefits for youth in and after foster care  
- Knowledge of timing and process for benefit applications  
- Service delivery approach must consider youth's custom needs and be adaptable to age and individual developmental needs  
- Youth must be aware of expectations of this Add-On Service

**Required Support Activities:** In collaboration with SSCC/DFPS PAL staff: Casey Life Skills Assessments completion, Regular PAL program event attendance, PAL Life Skills Training completion, Participation in after school/extracurricular activities as directed by youth, Youth Leadership Council participation (if interested), Circles of Support/transition planning meeting attendance, Extended Foster Care/Transitional Living program navigation support

**Service Plan Components:** Include at minimum: Status of state/federal benefit applications or guardianship eligibility, Driver's license plan (including driver's education and auto insurance) or state ID as appropriate

**Foster Family Home Credentialing:** Additional assessment required beyond T3C Basic credentialing including experience working with transitioning youth, understanding of independence preparation requirements, capacity to support educational and employment goals, commitment to transition-specific training and support

### 8\. ☑ Kinship Caregiver Support Services

For children placed in kinship Foster Family Homes receiving Kinship Caregiver Support Services Add-On, Refuge House provides specialized support recognizing the unique dynamics of relative caregiving relationships.

**Staff Expertise Requirements:**

- Staff must have expertise in Kinship Care including understanding of state and federal benefits that Kinship Caregivers may be eligible to receive  
- Knowledge of timing and process to complete/submit applications for TANF for relative caregivers, Kinship Care payments, and other assistance programs  
- Understanding of verification requirements and expedited processing procedures

**Service Delivery Approach:**

- Must consider custom needs of youth, Caregivers, and physical residence  
- Must be adaptable, including working on weekends and outside normal business hours  
- Delivered in conjunction with youth's primary Service Package  
- Staff available 24 hours a day/7 days a week to provide immediate response to Kinship Foster Family Home Caregivers

**Aftercare Services (San Antonio only):** As part of aftercare program, provide or refer Kinship Caregivers to support groups, include support group information in Aftercare Services plan at discharge, track participation and outcomes

**Foster Family Home Credentialing:** Additional assessment required beyond T3C Basic credentialing including experience with relative caregiving dynamics, understanding of permanency options specific to kinship care, capacity for managing complex family relationships, commitment to kinship-specific training and support

### 9\. ☑ Pregnant & Parenting Youth or Young Adult Support Services

For pregnant and parenting youth receiving this Add-On Service, Refuge House provides dual-generation support addressing the unique needs of both the young parent and their child(ren).

**Youth Notification:** Youth will be notified of expectations of Add-On Service program including participation requirements and available supports

**Custom Parenting Plan Components:** Include the following areas: Safe sleeping arrangements, Childproofing suggestions for potentially dangerous home settings, Child development and methods to cope with challenging behaviors, Selection of appropriate substitute caregivers, Benefits of reading, singing, and talking to young children, Healthy Relationships, including intimate partner violence prevention

**Dual-Generation Support:** Foster Family Home Caregivers offer logistical support, transportation, coordination, and documentation/record keeping for both youth and their child(ren)

**Service Plan Requirements:** Include support and aid in: Completing all necessary referrals for youth and child(ren), Coordination of services including Early Childhood Intervention, HHSC Women and Children's Health programs, Prevention and Early Intervention Program, Day care (if applicable), All other state, federal, and community benefits, Assistance with completing all forms and referrals

**Foster Family Home Credentialing:** Additional assessment required beyond T3C Basic credentialing including experience with infant and child care, understanding of dual-generation service needs, capacity to support both youth and child development, commitment to parenting education and support

## DEFINITIONS:

**CANS 3.0:** Child and Adolescent Needs and Strengths assessment tool version 3.0 used to identify strengths and needs for service planning

**Evidence-informed Treatment Model:** TBRI® (Trust-Based Relational Intervention®) \- trauma-informed approach emphasizing connection, empowerment, and correction

**Foster Family Home Credentialing:** Process to assess and approve individual foster homes to provide specific T3C Service Package services beyond basic DFPS licensing

**PAL Program:** Preparation for Adult Living program providing life skills training and support for youth transitioning to independence

**Service Package:** T3C Basic Foster Family Home Support Services Package providing community-based foster care with enhanced support services

**TBRI®:** Trust-Based Relational Intervention® \- evidence-based, trauma-informed intervention designed to meet the complex needs of vulnerable children
