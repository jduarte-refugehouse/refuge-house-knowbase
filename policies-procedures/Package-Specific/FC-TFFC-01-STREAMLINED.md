## PURPOSE

To establish Refuge House's commitment to providing the highest level of clinical intervention in a family setting under the T3C Treatment Foster Family Care Support Services Package. This intensive, time-limited service addresses children with serious emotional, conduct, or behavioral disorders requiring concentrated therapeutic intervention.

## POLICY

Refuge House provides Treatment Foster Family Care through highly-trained, credentialed foster homes using Trust-Based Relational Intervention® (TBRI®) as our Evidence-informed Treatment Model, enhanced with intensive clinical interventions. This package serves children requiring the highest level of clinical intervention available in a family setting to perform day-to-day activities.

**Legal Framework:** These services adhere to Texas Family Code Section 264.1073 and Texas Administrative Code Rule §700.1335.

---

## SERVICE PACKAGE REQUIREMENTS

### 1. Admission Criteria

**Primary Requirement:** DSM-5 diagnosis for emotional, conduct, or behavioral disorder

**Plus Two or More of the Following:**
- Major self-injurious actions, including suicide attempt within last 12 months
- Difficulties presenting significant risk of harm to others, including frequent or unpredictable physical aggression
- Additional DSM-5 diagnosis of substance-related and/or addictive disorder with severe impairment

**Admission Process:** Per FC2-01.1 Admission Assessment Procedure and FC6-01 Admission Matching & Criteria Policy, with Treatment Director clinical review to verify DSM-5 diagnosis plus two additional criteria.

### 2. Capacity Limitation

**Maximum 2 children** in foster care per home due to intensity of services.

### 3. Time-Limited Service Parameters

| Parameter | Duration |
|-----------|----------|
| Standard Treatment | Up to 274 days |
| Extension (if needed) | Up to 91 additional days |
| **Maximum Total** | **365 days** |

**CRITICAL:** Individual child cannot be served under this package for more than 365 days. Step-down planning begins at admission.

### 4. Service Planning Requirements

Per FC3-01.1 Individual Service Planning Procedure with the following package-specific modifications:

| Requirement | Timeline |
|-------------|----------|
| Initial Service Plan | Within 30 days |
| Service Plan Reviews | **Every 60 days** (vs. 90 days for Mental Health/IDD) |
| CANS 3.0 | Every 90 days |

**Service Plan Must Include:**
- Step-down readiness assessment at each review
- Specific clinical interventions and frequencies
- Therapy frequency justified in writing

### 5. Continued Stay Requirements (60-Day Reviews)

**CRITICAL REQUIREMENT:** At each 60-day Service Plan review, considering most recent CANS 3.0:

| Who | Requirement |
|-----|-------------|
| **Program Director** | Must provide written confirmation that child continues to meet criteria and less-restrictive placement is not appropriate |
| **Treatment Director** | Must provide written confirmation that child is benefitting from Treatment Model and less-restrictive placement is not appropriate |

**BOTH signatures required.** One signature alone is insufficient.

**Documentation:** Written confirmation in case record; copy to SSCC/DFPS within **15 business days** of review.

**Step-Down Determination:** Each review assesses whether child can transition to:
- Same home under Basic Foster Family Home Package (if dually credentialed)
- Different home under Basic or other appropriate Service Package
- Permanency placement

### 6. Therapeutic Services

Child must receive **regular and frequent** therapeutic services:
- Individual therapy per clinical need (typically weekly or more)
- Family therapy as clinically indicated
- Group therapy as clinically indicated

Services provided by Licensed Therapist specializing in serious mental, emotional, and/or behavioral disorders. Therapy frequency must be justified in writing in Service Plan.

### 7. Staffing Ratios (Intensive)

| Position | Ratio | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Case Manager | **1:6** | More intensive than Mental Health (1:15) |
| Licensed Therapist | **1:11** | |
| Behavior Support Specialist/Mentor | **1:6** | More intensive than Mental Health (1:15) |
| Crisis Management Staff | 1:25 | 24/7 availability |
| Aftercare Case Manager | 1:25 | Mandatory aftercare |

### 8. Foster Family Home Requirements

**Dual Credentialing Required:** Foster home AND CPA must be credentialed for BOTH Treatment Foster Family Care AND T3C Basic Foster Family Home Support Services. This allows step-down to Basic Package in same home when appropriate.

**Additional Training:** Per TAC §749.863(c), caregivers must complete **20 hours additional training** for Treatment Foster Care, including:
- Managing high-acuity behaviors
- Implementing intensive behavior support plans
- Crisis intervention and de-escalation
- Supporting concentrated therapeutic treatment

**Caregiver Requirements:**
- Ability to attend multiple meetings per week
- Respond immediately when child is in crisis
- Participate in therapy with child as needed

### 9. Specialized Paid Intermittent Alternative Care

Required dedicated program with **1 skilled Intermittent Alternative Care Caregiver for every 20 children** receiving Treatment Foster Family Care. Designed to ensure caregiver wellness given intensive nature of package.

### 10. Mandatory Aftercare Services (6 Months)

**CRITICAL:** Treatment Foster Family Care REQUIRES 6 months of Aftercare Services.

Per FC-AF-01 Aftercare Services Policy with the following package-specific schedule:

| Period | Minimum Contact Frequency |
|--------|--------------------------|
| **Month 1** | **Weekly** |
| **Months 2-6** | **Twice monthly** |

Additional ad-hoc meetings and referrals as needed.

**Funding:** Built into daily foster care rate; no separate payment for aftercare.

**Documentation:** Monthly reports to SSCC/DFPS documenting all contacts, referrals, and case management support.

### 11. Discharge and Transition

Per FC14-01 Discharge and Permanency Planning Policy with time-limited focus:

**Step-Down Planning from Day 1:** Due to time-limited nature, discharge planning begins at admission with preliminary step-down goals in initial Service Plan.

**Transition Options:**
- Same home under Basic Package (if dually credentialed and child stable)
- Different home under Basic or other appropriate Package
- Permanency placement

## QUALITY ASSURANCE

Quality Assurance and Continued Stay Guidelines per T3C Blueprint include:

- Ongoing adjustment based on CANS 3.0 and Service Plan
- Primary admission reason continues or replaced with aligned service needs
- Services support safety, well-being, and permanency
- Less-restrictive placement confirmed inappropriate at each 60-day review
- Both Program Director AND Treatment Director written confirmation required
- Foster home maintains Treatment Foster Care credential

Outcome tracking per CQI Plan (revised-cqi-plan.md) including post-discharge stability at 30/60/90/180 days.

---

## DEFINITIONS

**Treatment Foster Family Care:** Highest level of clinical intervention in family setting for children with DSM-5 diagnosis plus two additional criteria, requiring intensive, time-limited (365-day maximum) therapeutic services.

**Step-Down:** Planned transition from Treatment Foster Family Care to less-restrictive placement based on treatment progress and readiness assessment.

**Dual Credentialing:** Requirement that CPA and foster home maintain credentials for both Treatment Foster Family Care and Basic Foster Family Home packages.
