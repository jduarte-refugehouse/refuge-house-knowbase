# POLICY SECTIONS

## 1. PURPOSE AND SERVICE PACKAGE DESCRIPTION

### 1.1 Service Package Definition

T3C Treatment Foster Family Care Support Services is the **highest level of clinical intervention** offered in a family setting, providing concentrated therapeutic services for children with serious mental, emotional, and/or behavioral challenges who require intensive, time-limited treatment.

### 1.2 Target Population

Children, youth, and young adults who meet **ALL** of the following criteria:

**Primary Requirement:**
- DSM-5 diagnosis (or ICD-10 equivalent) of serious mental, emotional, and/or behavioral disorder

**PLUS at least TWO of the following:**
- Behaviors that place child or others at risk
- History of multiple placements
- History of psychiatric hospitalization
- Behaviors requiring intensive therapeutic intervention
- Need for concentrated support to prevent more restrictive placement

### 1.3 Treatment Philosophy

Refuge House recognizes that:

- **Intensive intervention** can achieve stabilization for step-down to less restrictive settings
- **Time-limited treatment** (365-day maximum) ensures focused, goal-directed services
- **Family-based treatment** is preferable to institutional care when appropriate supports are provided
- **Step-down planning from Day 1** ensures children progress to least restrictive appropriate setting

### 1.4 Service Package Characteristics

**CRITICAL DISTINCTIONS:**

| Characteristic | Treatment Foster Family Care | Mental & Behavioral Health (comparison) |
|----------------|------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Time Limit | **365 days maximum** | No maximum |
| Continued Stay Reviews | **Every 60 days** | Every 90 days |
| Review Signature | **DUAL (Program Director + Treatment Director)** | Single |
| Case Manager Ratio | **1:6** | 1:15 |
| BSS/Mentor Ratio | **1:6** | 1:15 |
| Therapist Ratio | **1:11** | 1:14 |
| Foster Home Capacity | **Maximum 2 children** | Per home study |
| Foster Parent Training | **20 hours specialized** | Standard |
| Aftercare | **Mandatory 6 months** | Required |
| Step-Down Planning | **From Day 1** | As indicated |
| Dual Credentialing | **Required (TFFC + Basic)** | Not required |

## 3. ADMISSION CRITERIA AND GUIDELINES

### 3.1 Admission Eligibility

**All THREE criteria must be met:**

1. **DSM-5 Diagnosis:** Child has documented DSM-5 diagnosis (or ICD-10 equivalent) of serious mental, emotional, and/or behavioral disorder

2. **At Least TWO Additional Criteria:**
   - Behaviors that place child or others at risk
   - History of multiple placements
   - History of psychiatric hospitalization
   - Behaviors requiring intensive therapeutic intervention
   - Need for concentrated support to prevent more restrictive placement

3. **Appropriateness for Family Setting:** Clinical determination that child can be safely served in family-based setting with intensive supports

## 8. QUALITY ASSURANCE AND CONTINUED STAY GUIDELINES

### 8.1 60-Day Continued Stay Reviews

**CRITICAL:** Reviews occur every **60 days** (vs. 90 days for other packages) with **DUAL SIGNATURE** requirement.

### 8.2 Dual Signature Requirement

> **ðŸ“‹ DERIVED FROM: T3C Blueprint p.145**
>
> At each 60-day Service Plan review, considering most recent CANS 3.0:
>
> | Reviewer | Required Confirmation |
> |----------|----------------------|
> | **Program Director** | Written confirmation that child continues to meet criteria AND less-restrictive placement is not appropriate |
> | **Treatment Director** | Written confirmation that child is benefitting from Treatment Model AND less-restrictive placement is not appropriate |
>
> **BOTH signatures required.** One signature alone is insufficient.
>
> **Documentation:** Written confirmation in case record; copy to SSCC/DFPS within **15 business days** of review.

### 8.3 Continued Stay Guidelines

Quality Assurance and Continued Stay Guidelines per T3C Blueprint include:

- Ongoing adjustment based on CANS 3.0 and Service Plan
- Primary admission reason continues or replaced with aligned service needs
- Services support safety, well-being, and permanency
- Less-restrictive placement confirmed inappropriate at each 60-day review
- Both Program Director AND Treatment Director written confirmation required
- Foster home maintains Treatment Foster Care credential

### 8.4 Step-Down Determination

At each 60-day review, assess whether child can transition to:

- Same home under Basic Foster Family Home Package (if dually credentialed and child stable)
- Different home under Basic or other appropriate Service Package
- Permanency placement

# REFERENCE SECTIONS

## 21. DEFINITIONS

**Dual Credentialing:** Requirement that CPA and foster home maintain credentials for both Treatment Foster Family Care AND Basic Foster Family Home packages to enable step-down in same home.

**Dual Signature Confirmation:** Requirement that both Program Director and Treatment Director provide written confirmation of continued need for T3C Treatment Foster Family Care at each 60-day Service Plan review per T3C Blueprint p.145.

**On-Call Licensed Therapist:** Licensed Therapist trained in CPA's Evidence-informed Treatment Model (TBRIÂ®) available 24/7 for crisis consultation and response per T3C Blueprint p.138.

**Step-Down:** Planned transition from Treatment Foster Family Care to less-restrictive placement based on treatment progress and readiness assessment.

**Treatment Foster Family Care:** Highest level of clinical intervention in family setting for children with DSM-5 diagnosis plus two additional criteria, requiring intensive, time-limited (365-day maximum) therapeutic services.
