## PURPOSE

This policy establishes Refuge House's approach to providing enhanced support services for kinship foster families through the T3C Kinship Caregiver Support Services Add-On. Kinship placements present unique opportunities and challenges, requiring specialized support to navigate complex family dynamics while maintaining the child's natural family connections and cultural identity.

The purpose of this add-on service is to strengthen kinship placements by providing customized assistance to caregivers and children, including support through the verification process and ongoing specialized services that address the distinct needs of relative caregivers.

## POLICY STATEMENT

Refuge House is committed to supporting kinship caregivers who provide care for children in foster care by:

1. **Recognizing the unique dynamics** of kinship placements, including pre-existing relationships, changed family roles, and complex emotional bonds  
     
2. **Providing specialized support** that addresses the specific challenges of kinship care, including boundary establishment, role transitions, and family system adjustments  
     
3. **Ensuring cultural responsiveness** in all kinship support services, recognizing that kinship care often preserves cultural identity and family traditions  
     
4. **Supporting family connections** while helping kinship caregivers maintain appropriate boundaries with biological parents and extended family  
     
5. **Delivering trauma-informed services** that acknowledge the impact of trauma on both the child and the kinship family system  
     
6. **Maintaining flexibility** in service delivery, including availability outside normal business hours to accommodate kinship caregivers' schedules  
     
7. **Ensuring staff expertise** in kinship care, including comprehensive knowledge of state and federal benefits available to kinship caregivers and the application processes required

## CORE SERVICE COMPONENTS

### 1\. Enhanced Assessment Services

Refuge House will conduct comprehensive assessments of kinship placements that evaluate:

- Pre-existing relationship dynamics between child and caregiver  
- Family system functioning and role adjustments  
- Support network availability and gaps  
- Training and resource needs specific to kinship care  
- Cultural factors impacting the placement  
- Financial needs and benefit eligibility

### 2\. Verification Process Support

Refuge House will provide intensive support during the foster home verification process, recognizing that kinship caregivers often face unique challenges in meeting licensing requirements. This support includes:

- Navigation assistance through licensing requirements  
- Documentation support and guidance  
- Expedited processing coordination with DFPS to minimize placement disruption  
- Financial assistance coordination during the verification period  
- Advocacy with licensing and placement authorities  
- Training schedule flexibility to accommodate caregiver work and family obligations

### 3\. Staff Expertise Requirements

All staff providing Kinship Caregiver Support Services will possess demonstrated expertise in kinship care. This expertise specifically includes:

- Comprehensive understanding of state and federal benefits that kinship caregivers may be eligible to receive while caring for youth in care  
- Knowledge of the timing for and process required to complete and submit applications or other necessary documentation to obtain assistance  
- Understanding of programs including but not limited to:  
  - TANF for relative caregivers  
  - Kinship Care payments  
  - Permanency Care Assistance (PCA)  
  - Medicaid and CHIP enrollment  
  - SNAP benefits  
  - Social Security survivor or disability benefits when applicable

### 4\. Adaptable Service Delivery Approach

The approach and delivery of Kinship Caregiver Support Services will:

- Consider the custom needs of the youth, the caregivers, and the physical residence  
- Be adaptable to support and sustain a safe verified kinship foster home placement  
- Include working with caregivers on weekends and outside normal business hours  
- Be delivered in conjunction with the youth's primary Service Package  
- Utilize flexible meeting locations and methods based on caregiver preferences

### 5\. 24/7 Support Availability

Recognizing the unique challenges of kinship care, Refuge House will ensure:

- Staff available 24 hours a day/7 days a week to provide immediate response to Kinship Foster Family Home Caregivers  
- Crisis response capability for emergent situations  
- After-hours support for placement preservation  
- Weekend and evening availability for routine support services

### 6\. Ongoing Specialized Support

Kinship caregivers will receive enhanced ongoing support services including:

- Boundary establishment coaching using evidence-based approaches  
- Family mediation and conflict resolution services as needed  
- Grief and loss support related to role transitions  
- Connection to kinship-specific support groups  
- Benefits navigation and advocacy  
- Respite care coordination when available  
- Transportation assistance for essential appointments  
- Educational advocacy and support

### 7\. Training and Education

Refuge House will provide specialized training for kinship caregivers addressing:

- Understanding trauma's impact on family relationships  
- Managing complex family dynamics and divided loyalties  
- Navigating dual relationships with biological parents  
- Legal aspects of kinship care and permanency options  
- Accessing and maintaining state and federal benefits  
- Self-care and stress management for kinship caregivers

### 8\. Staff Training Enhancement Curriculum

Recognizing that all staff may encounter kinship placements, Refuge House will implement a comprehensive staff training enhancement curriculum to ensure organization-wide readiness to support kinship families. This curriculum will prepare all staff to:

- Recognize and respond to the unique needs of kinship placements  
- Provide initial support and appropriate referrals  
- Understand benefit eligibility and application basics  
- Maintain cultural sensitivity in kinship situations  
- Support the primary kinship team when needed

The enhanced curriculum ensures that kinship families receive knowledgeable support from their first contact with any Refuge House staff member, creating a seamless support experience throughout our organization.

### 9\. Aftercare Services

Following permanency achievement or placement changes, Refuge House will provide comprehensive aftercare services for six months including:

**Months 1-3:** Weekly contact through calls or in-person meetings with crisis intervention support as needed

**Months 4-6:** Twice monthly follow-up calls and/or meetings with flexible scheduling to accommodate youth/family availability

**Documentation Requirements:** All contacts or attempted contacts, referrals, and other case management support provided during the aftercare service period will be documented in the child's case record. This detailed documentation will be submitted to the SSCC/DFPS caseworker at the end of each month.

**Support Group Connections:** As part of the aftercare program, Refuge House will provide or refer kinship caregivers to support groups. Support group information will be included in the Aftercare Services plan provided at the time of discharge.

## ASSESSMENT AND CREDENTIALING PROCESS

Refuge House will maintain policies and procedures for assessing and credentialing Foster Family Homes for the Kinship Caregiver Support Services Add-On. This process includes:

1. Initial screening to determine appropriateness for kinship placement  
2. Comprehensive assessment of caregiver capacity and support needs  
3. Evaluation of family dynamics and potential challenges  
4. Review of financial stability and benefit eligibility  
5. Assessment of training needs and learning preferences  
6. Development of support plan tailored to kinship placement needs

## ELIGIBILITY AND REFERRAL

Children eligible for kinship caregiver support services include those placed with:

- Biological relatives (grandparents, aunts, uncles, adult siblings, etc.)  
- Fictive kin with established relationships  
- Other adults with significant pre-existing relationships

Referrals will be accepted from:

- DFPS/SSCC placement units  
- Current kinship caregivers seeking additional support  
- Prospective kinship caregivers during verification

## QUALITY ASSURANCE

Refuge House will maintain continuous quality improvement through:

1. **Outcome Tracking** including placement stability rates, permanency achievement, and caregiver satisfaction  
     
2. **Data Analysis** by individual kinship home, support staff caseloads, and service utilization patterns  
     
3. **Logic Model Maintenance** specific to kinship support services, updated based on CQI findings

## DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

All kinship support services will be thoroughly documented in the child's case record using Radius case management system. Documentation will include:

- Initial and ongoing assessments  
- Kinship Support Plans  
- All support activities and contacts  
- Training participation  
- Aftercare services  
- Outcome measures

The kinship dashboard will provide centralized access to resources, guides, and tracking tools specific to kinship placements.

## COORDINATION WITH OTHER SERVICES

Kinship support services will be fully integrated with:

- The child's primary service package  
- Family engagement and visitation planning  
- Permanency planning efforts  
- Medical and therapeutic services  
- Educational support services

## DEFINITIONS

**Fictive Kin**: Individuals with significant emotional ties to a child but no biological relationship

**Kinship Caregiver**: A relative or person with whom a child has a pre-existing relationship who provides foster care

**PMC with PCA**: Permanent Managing Conservatorship with Permanency Care Assistance

**Verification Process**: The licensing process for kinship caregivers to become verified foster homes

**24/7 Availability**: Staff accessibility through on-call system for immediate response
