# **POLICY SECTIONS**

## **1\. PURPOSE AND COMMITMENT**

The Board of Directors establishes this policy to ensure pregnant and parenting youth in our care receive comprehensive, trauma-informed support services that promote healthy outcomes for both the young parent and their child(ren). We commit to providing non-judgmental, strength-based services that honor the youth's role as a parent while supporting their continued development and education.

This policy governs the implementation of the Pregnant & Parenting Youth Support Services Add-On as defined in the T3C System Blueprint, ensuring full compliance with all regulatory requirements while maintaining our commitment to exceeding minimum standards through evidence-based practice.

## **2\. GOVERNING PRINCIPLES**

### **2.1 Dual-Generation Approach**

We recognize that supporting young parents requires simultaneous attention to the needs of both the parent and child. All services shall address the intergenerational impact of trauma while building protective factors for both generations.

### **2.2 Non-Judgmental Support**

We affirm that young parents deserve respect, dignity, and support regardless of their circumstances. Services shall be delivered without judgment, focusing on building parenting capacity and confidence.

### **2.3 Voluntary Engagement**

We acknowledge that services cannot be forced. Our approach emphasizes persistent, creative engagement strategies that build trust and demonstrate the value of support services.

### **2.4 Cultural Responsiveness**

We honor diverse parenting practices and family structures, ensuring services respect cultural values while promoting child safety and well-being.

### **2.5 Father Inclusion**

We commit to inclusive services that support both mothers and fathers, recognizing the importance of all parents in children's lives.

### **2.6 Program Expectations Notification**

We commit to ensuring all youth receiving this Add-On Service are formally notified of program expectations, participation requirements, and available supports in a manner that is accessible and youth-friendly.

## **3\. SERVICE PHILOSOPHY**

Our pregnant and parenting youth services embrace the belief that young parents can succeed with appropriate support. We provide:

- Comprehensive support addressing both parent and child needs  
- Trauma-informed care recognizing intergenerational impacts  
- Practical assistance with daily parenting challenges  
- Educational support enabling continued personal growth  
- Connection to community resources and benefits  
- Mentorship fostering parenting confidence  
- Planning for sustainable independence  
- Logistical support, transportation, and coordination for both youth and their children

## **5\. ELIGIBILITY AND ENROLLMENT**

### **Purpose and Overview**

Clear eligibility determination ensures appropriate youth receive specialized support services. This section establishes procedures for identifying eligible youth and managing enrollment in compliance with T3C Blueprint requirements.

### **5.1 Eligibility Criteria**

| Who | How | When | Where | Regulatory Reference |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Case Manager | Review youth status against eligibility criteria: pregnant or actively parenting biological child | Upon referral or identification | Case management system | T3C Blueprint p.158-164 |
| Case Manager | Verify child custody status (DFPS conservatorship or not) | Within 24 hours of identification | Legal records | T3C Blueprint p.158 |
| Case Manager | Confirm youth resides with biological child in foster home | At placement | Placement records | T3C Blueprint p.158 |
| Supervisor | Approve eligibility determination | Within 2 business days | Supervision log | TAC §749.1301 |

**Two Eligibility Scenarios:**

1. DFPS does NOT have conservatorship of youth's child  
2. DFPS has conservatorship with active reunification goal

## **7\. ASSESSMENT AND PLANNING**

### **Purpose and Overview**

Comprehensive assessment captures the unique needs of both young parent and child, forming the foundation for individualized support planning. Separate timelines for pregnant versus parenting youth ensure appropriate urgency.

### **7.1 Initial Assessment Process**

| Who | How | When | Where | Regulatory Reference |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Parenting Support Specialist | Complete pregnancy assessment addressing prenatal care, support system, knowledge gaps | **Within 7 days** for pregnant youth | Assessment form in Radius | T3C Blueprint p.159 |
| Parenting Support Specialist | Complete parenting assessment including parent-child interaction, skills, supports | **Within 14 days** for parenting youth | Assessment form in Radius | T3C Blueprint p.159 |
| Youth | Participate in strength-based assessment process | During assessment | Assessment documentation | TAC §749.1291 |
| Parenting Support Specialist | Observe parent-child interaction in natural setting | Within assessment timeframe | Observation notes | T3C Blueprint p.159 |

### **7.2 Custom Parenting Plan Development**

| Who | How | When | Where | Regulatory Reference |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Parenting Support Specialist | **Develop Custom Parenting Plan** that includes all required areas: **• Safe sleeping arrangements** • **Suggestions for childproofing potentially dangerous settings in a home** • **Child development and methods to cope with challenging behaviors** • **Selection of appropriate substitute caregivers** • **The benefits of reading, singing, and talking to young children** • **Healthy relationships, including the prevention of intimate partner violence** | **Within 7 days of assessment** | Service plan supplement in Radius | **T3C Blueprint p.159-161 \- ENHANCEMENT REQUIREMENT** |
| Youth | Identify learning priorities and preferred methods | During planning | Plan document | TAC §749.1291 |
| Treatment Team | Review and integrate parenting plan with service plan | At next review | Service plan | TAC §749.1309 |
| Foster Parent | Provide input on daily support needs | During planning | Team notes | TAC §749.2457 |

**Required Plan Components (per T3C Blueprint p.159-161):**

- Safe sleeping arrangements (including SIDS prevention, crib safety, room sharing guidelines)  
- Childproofing suggestions for kitchen, bathroom, stairs, outlets, and other dangerous settings  
- Child development milestones and methods to cope with challenging behaviors  
- Selection criteria and screening process for appropriate substitute caregivers  
- Benefits of reading, singing, and talking to young children for brain development  
- Healthy relationships education including intimate partner violence prevention  
- Prenatal/postnatal care (if applicable)  
- Early brain development  
- Positive parenting techniques  
- Co-parenting strategies (if applicable)  
- Perinatal mood disorder support  
- Infant nutrition
