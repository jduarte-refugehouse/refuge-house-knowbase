## PURPOSE AND OVERVIEW

This procedure operationalizes the Kinship Caregiver Support Services Add-On Policy (FC-KIN-01) by providing detailed implementation steps for supporting kinship placements. As established in our policy, kinship placements "present unique opportunities and challenges, requiring specialized support to navigate complex family dynamics while maintaining the child's natural family connections and cultural identity."

The T3C Blueprint recognizes that kinship caregivers face distinct challenges including role transitions, boundary management, and complex relationships with biological parents. This procedure ensures systematic delivery of enhanced support services that strengthen these valuable placements while maintaining compliance with all regulatory requirements.

## RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN POLICY AND PROCEDURE

This procedure implements the policy's commitment to providing specialized kinship support through structured protocols that ensure consistent, high-quality service delivery. While the policy establishes our philosophical approach and service commitments, this procedure details the specific actions, timelines, and responsibilities required for implementation.

### 2\. REFERRAL AND INITIAL RESPONSE PROCEDURES

#### Purpose and Overview

Timely response to kinship referrals is critical for placement stability and caregiver success. The verification process can be overwhelming for relatives suddenly thrust into a caregiving role. This section establishes protocols for immediate response and support initiation.

#### 2.1 Referral Processing

| Who | How | When | Where | Regulatory Reference |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Intake Coordinator | 1\. Receive kinship referral via phone, email, or placement system 2\. Document in Radius: \- Child information \- Kinship caregiver details \- Relationship to child \- Immediate support needs \- Verification status | **Within 2 hours of referral** | Radius intake module | T3C Blueprint p.154-158 |
| Intake Coordinator | 2\. Assign to Kinship Support Worker based on: \- Geographic location \- Current caseload \- Language/cultural match when possible \- Complexity factors | **Within 4 hours of referral** | Staff assignment in Radius | T3C Blueprint p.154-158 |
| Kinship Support Worker | 3\. Make initial contact to: \- Introduce services \- Schedule initial meeting (including evenings/weekends) \- Identify urgent needs \- Begin relationship building | **Within 24 hours of assignment** | Contact documented in Radius | TAC §749.1001 |
| Kinship Support Worker | 4\. Coordinate with DFPS/SSCC to obtain: \- Placement authorization \- Child's essential documents \- Medical information \- Educational records | **Within 48 hours of referral** | Communication log | RCC Contract Term 7 |

#### 2.2 Emergency Support Activation

| Who | How | When | Where | Regulatory Reference |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Kinship Support Worker | 1\. Assess immediate needs including: \- Basic necessities (beds, clothing, food) \- Safety equipment \- Medical supplies \- School enrollment needs | **During initial contact** | Needs assessment in Radius | TAC §749.1001 |

## EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

In case of after-hours emergencies:

1. Caregiver calls 24/7 hotline  
2. On-call staff responds within 15 minutes  
3. Crisis intervention provided by phone  
4. In-person response dispatched if needed  
5. Follow-up within 24 hours by primary worker  
6. All contacts documented in case record
