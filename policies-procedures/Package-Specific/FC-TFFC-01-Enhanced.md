# POLICY SECTIONS

## 1. PURPOSE AND SERVICE PACKAGE DESCRIPTION

### 1.1 Purpose

This policy establishes Refuge House's commitment to providing the highest level of clinical intervention in a family setting under the T3C Treatment Foster Family Care Support Services Package.

### 1.2 Service Package Description

Treatment Foster Family Care provides intensive, time-limited therapeutic services for children with serious emotional, conduct, or behavioral disorders who require concentrated clinical intervention but can be safely served in a specialized foster home.

**Target Population:** Children who meet admission criteria demonstrating need for highest level of clinical intervention available in a family setting to perform day-to-day activities.

### 1.3 Relationship to Other Service Packages

This package represents the highest intensity of foster care services. Children may step down to:
- T3C Basic Foster Family Home Support Services (same home if dually credentialed, or different home)
- Other appropriate Service Package
- Permanency placement

---

## 2. GOVERNING PRINCIPLES

### 2.1 TBRI® Foundation with Intensive Clinical Enhancement

**Cross-Reference:** Refuge House implements TBRI® as our Evidence-informed Treatment Model per **FC-T3C-01 T3C Basic Foster Family Home Support Services Policy** and **TBRI Treatment Model Executive Summary**.

For Treatment Foster Family Care, TBRI principles are enhanced with intensive clinical interventions:

**Connecting Principles:** Intensive relationship-building to address severe attachment disruptions and trust deficits resulting from complex trauma.

**Empowering Principles:** Comprehensive regulation support for children with severe emotional dysregulation, including crisis prevention and intensive behavioral support.

**Correcting Principles:** Highly structured, therapeutic environment with immediate access to clinical intervention when behavioral escalation occurs.

### 2.2 Time-Limited Treatment Philosophy

This package recognizes that intensive intervention should achieve stabilization within defined timeframes, with step-down planning integrated from Day 1.

---

## 3. ADMISSION CRITERIA AND GUIDELINES

### 3.1 Admission Eligibility

**Primary Requirement:** DSM-5 diagnosis for emotional, conduct, or behavioral disorder

**PLUS Two or More of the Following:**
- Major self-injurious actions, including suicide attempt within last 12 months
- Difficulties presenting significant risk of harm to others, including frequent or unpredictable physical aggression
- Additional DSM-5 diagnosis of substance-related and/or addictive disorder with severe impairment

### 3.2 Admission Process

**Cross-Reference:** Admission follows **FC1-01.1 Admission Screening Procedure** and **FC2-01.1 Admission Assessment Procedure** with the following package-specific requirements:

| Who | How | When | Regulatory Reference |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Treatment Director | 1. Review clinical documentation 2. Verify DSM-5 diagnosis 3. Confirm child meets 2+ additional criteria 4. Document clinical appropriateness | Before placement | T3C Blueprint p.141-142; FC2-01.1 |

**Per FC6-01 Admission Matching & Criteria Policy** with additional requirements:
- Child Placing Agency AND Foster Family Home must be Credentialed for Treatment Foster Family Care
- Dual credentialing verified (Treatment Foster Care AND Basic Foster Home)
- Home capacity verified (maximum 2 children)
- Pre-placement visit conducted when appropriate

### 3.3 Capacity Limitation

**CRITICAL:** Foster homes providing Treatment Foster Family Care may have **no more than two (2) children in foster care** at the same time due to the intensive nature of services.

---

## 4. LENGTH OF SERVICE PARAMETERS

### 4.1 Time-Limited Service

| Parameter | Duration |
|-----------|----------|
| Standard Treatment Period | Up to 274 days |
| Extension (if clinically necessary) | Up to 91 additional days |
| **Maximum Total** | **365 days** |

**CRITICAL:** An individual child cannot be served under this package for more than 365 days.

### 4.2 Anticipated Length of Service

Refuge House policy must include anticipated Length of Service for children served under this package, recognizing that intensive intervention should achieve stabilization for step-down within defined timeframes.

### 4.3 Step-Down Planning from Day 1

Due to time-limited nature, step-down planning begins at admission with preliminary step-down goals included in initial Service Plan.

---

## 5. SERVICE PACKAGE EXPECTATIONS

### 5.1 Therapeutic Services

Child must receive **regular and frequent** therapeutic services:
- Individual therapy (weekly minimum, frequency per clinical need)
- Family therapy (as clinically indicated)
- Group therapy (as clinically indicated)

**Cross-Reference:** Services coordinated through **FC-STAR-01.1 STAR Health Coordination Procedure**. Therapy frequency must be justified in writing in Service Plan.

**Therapist Qualifications:** Licensed Therapist specializing in serious mental, emotional, and/or behavioral disorders.

### 5.2 Service Planning Requirements

**Cross-Reference:** Service planning follows **FC3-01.1 Individual Service Planning Procedure** with the following package-specific modifications:

| Requirement | Treatment Foster Care | Standard (per FC3-01.1) |
|-------------|----------------------|-------------------------|
| Service Plan Reviews | **Every 60 days** | Every 90 days (MH/IDD) |
| CANS 3.0 | Every 90 days | Every 90 days |
| Step-Down Assessment | At each 60-day review | Not applicable |

### 5.3 24/7 Crisis Availability

**Cross-Reference:** Crisis response per **Crisis Management Staffing Plan** with immediate access to clinical intervention when behavioral escalation occurs.

### 5.4 Human Trafficking Prevention

**Cross-Reference:** Per **FC-HT-01 Human Trafficking Prevention Policy** and **Human Trafficking Prevention Training Implementation Plan**.

### 5.5 Documentation and STAR Health Coordination

**Cross-Reference:** Documentation and service coordination per **FC-STAR-01.1 STAR Health Coordination Procedure** and **FC10-01 Physical and Mental Health Care Policy**.

### 5.6 Family Engagement

**Cross-Reference:** Family engagement per **FC7-01 Family Connections and Engagement Policy** with intensive family therapy as clinically indicated.

### 5.7 Educational Coordination

**Cross-Reference:** Educational coordination per **FC-T3C-01** with enhanced support for children with behavioral challenges in educational settings.

|----------|----------------------------|----------------------------------|

| Licensed Therapist | **1:11** | 1:14 |

### 6.4 Specialized Intermittent Alternative Care

**Package-Specific Requirement:** Dedicated program with **1 skilled Intermittent Alternative Care Caregiver for every 20 children** receiving Treatment Foster Family Care to ensure caregiver wellness.

### 6.5 Hours of Operation

Admissions and placement staff on-call/available **365 days per year, 24 hours per day**.

## 7. FOSTER FAMILY HOME REQUIREMENTS

### 7.1 Dual Credentialing Requirement

**CRITICAL:** Foster Family Home AND Child Placing Agency must be Credentialed for BOTH:
- T3C Treatment Foster Family Care Support Services Package
- T3C Basic Foster Family Home Support Services Package

This allows step-down to Basic Package in same home when appropriate.

### 7.2 Credentialing Process

**Cross-Reference:** Credentialing follows **FC-CRED-01.1 Foster Family Home Credentialing Procedure** with the following package-specific additions.

### 7.3 Additional Training Requirements

**Per TAC §749.863(c):** Caregivers must complete **20 hours additional training** for Treatment Foster Care beyond standard foster parent training, including:
- Managing high-acuity behaviors
- Implementing intensive behavior support plans
- Crisis intervention and de-escalation
- Supporting concentrated therapeutic treatment
- Recognizing and responding to psychiatric emergencies

**Cross-Reference:** Training per **FC-16 Caregiver Training Policy** and **T3C Staff and Caregiver Training Plan**.

### 7.4 Caregiver Competencies

Foster Family Home Caregivers must demonstrate ability to:
- Attend multiple meetings per week
- Respond immediately when child is in crisis
- Participate in therapy with child as needed
- Implement behavior support plans consistently
- Maintain structured therapeutic environment

### 7.5 Capacity Limitation

**Maximum 2 children in foster care** per home due to intensity of services.

### 7.6 Awake Night Supervision

Awake night supervision required in foster homes with 7 or more children, or as clinically indicated for specific children with nighttime safety concerns.

---

## 8. QUALITY ASSURANCE AND CONTINUED STAY GUIDELINES

### 8.1 60-Day Continued Stay Reviews

**Cross-Reference:** Continued Stay Reviews follow **FC-CSR-01.1 Continued Stay Review Procedure** with enhanced 60-day frequency and dual-signature requirement.

**CRITICAL REQUIREMENT:** At each 60-day Service Plan review, considering most recent CANS 3.0:

| Reviewer | Required Confirmation |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Program Director** | Written confirmation that child continues to meet criteria and less-restrictive placement is not appropriate |
| **Treatment Director** | Written confirmation that child is benefitting from Treatment Model and less-restrictive placement is not appropriate |

**BOTH signatures required.** Confirmation must be documented in case record and copy provided to SSCC/DFPS within **15 business days** of review.

### 8.2 Continued Stay Criteria

Quality Assurance and Continued Stay Guidelines include:
- Ongoing adjustment based on CANS 3.0 and Service Plan
- Primary admission reason continues or replaced with aligned service needs
- Services support safety, well-being, and permanency
- Less-restrictive placement confirmed inappropriate at each 60-day review
- Foster home maintains Treatment Foster Care credential

### 8.3 Desired Individual Outcomes

**Cross-Reference:** Outcome tracking per **FC-17 Continuous Quality Improvement Policy** and **CQI Plan** with treatment-specific metrics.

## 9. AFTERCARE SERVICES REQUIREMENTS

### 9.1 Mandatory 6-Month Aftercare

**CRITICAL:** Treatment Foster Family Care REQUIRES 6 months of Aftercare Services.

**Cross-Reference:** Aftercare follows **FC-AF-01 Aftercare Services Policy** with the following package-specific enhanced schedule:

| Period | Minimum Contact Frequency |
|--------|--------------------------|
| **Month 1** | **Weekly** (in-person, phone, or video) |
| **Months 2-6** | **Twice monthly** (in-person, phone, or video) |

Additional ad-hoc meetings and referrals as needed.

### 9.2 Aftercare Components

Per **FC-AF-01 Aftercare Services Policy**:
- Case management support
- Monitoring of step-down adjustment
- Crisis intervention as needed
- Coordination with receiving placement
- Connection to ongoing services

### 9.3 Aftercare Funding

Built into daily foster care rate; no separate payment for aftercare.

### 9.4 Aftercare Documentation

**Cross-Reference:** Per **FC-AF-01 Aftercare Services Policy**, monthly reports to SSCC/DFPS documenting all contacts, referrals, and case management support.

## 19. DEFINITIONS

**Dual Credentialing:** Requirement that CPA and foster home maintain credentials for BOTH Treatment Foster Family Care and Basic Foster Family Home packages.

**Step-Down:** Planned transition from Treatment Foster Family Care to less-restrictive placement based on treatment progress.

**Treatment Foster Family Care:** Highest level of clinical intervention in family setting for children with DSM-5 diagnosis plus two additional criteria, requiring intensive, time-limited (365-day maximum) therapeutic services.
