## PURPOSE:

To establish a standardized process for implementing alternative and substitute care options for children and youth in Refuge House foster homes, ensuring supervision that meets each child's individual needs while maintaining compliance with all regulatory requirements.

## RESPONSIBILITY:

- Caregivers  
- Case Managers  
- Program Directors/LCPAAs  
- Treatment Directors  
- Quality Assurance  
- Finance Manager  
- Accounting

## PROCEDURE:

### 1\. GENERAL SUBSTITUTE CARE REQUIREMENTS

#### Purpose and Overview

All forms of substitute care must prioritize the child's safety, well-being, and continuity of care. The policy requirement that substitute care providers be familiar with trauma-informed approaches and TBRI principles ensures children experience minimal disruption during these transitions. These general requirements apply to all forms of substitute care, from brief babysitting arrangements to extended IAC placements.

| Who | How | When | Where | Regulatory Reference |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Case Manager | 1\. Screen all potential substitute care providers according to: • Background check requirements • Training completion status • Ability to meet child's specific needs • Understanding of TBRI principles 2\. Document screening results 3\. Maintain approved provider list | Prior to any substitute care arrangement | Radius Provider Management | TAC §749.2627 |
| Substitute Care Provider | 1\. Follow these non-negotiable requirements: • No smoking in vehicle or home while providing care • No alcohol consumption while providing care • No illegal drug use • No profanity 2\. Maintain these care standards: • Provide supervision per child's needs • Only release child to authorized personnel • Maintain educational continuity • Provide required transportation • Respect child's identity and culture • Use TBRI principles for connection • Follow established routines • Adhere to all dietary requirements | Throughout substitute care period | Foster home or approved location | TAC §749.2629 |
| On-Call Staff | 1\. Maintain 24/7 availability 2\. Provide emergency support including: • Behavior management guidance • Medical emergency protocols • Incident reporting procedures • TBRI strategy coaching 3\. Document all contacts | 24/7 during substitute care | Phone/Text support | RCC Contract Term 10 |

### 2\. BABYSITTING

#### Purpose and Overview

Babysitting provides caregivers with brief respites for appointments, errands, or personal time while ensuring children remain in familiar surroundings. The policy limits babysitting to 8 consecutive hours without overnight care, recognizing that longer periods require more formal arrangements with fully verified providers.

| Who | How | When | Where | Regulatory Reference |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Caregiver | 1\. Verify babysitter meets all qualifications: • 18+ years (in-home) or 21+ years (outside home) • Current first-aid/CPR certification • Cleared background checks • Valid driver's license and insurance • Clear TB screening • Required training completed 2\. Prepare child using TBRI connecting principles 3\. Provide babysitter with: • Emergency contacts • Child's routine and preferences • Behavioral strategies • Medication schedule if applicable | Before each babysitting occurrence | Foster home | TAC §749.2621 |
| Case Manager | 1\. Maintain current Babysitter Information Form 2\. Verify credentials annually 3\. Track usage to ensure compliance with: • 8-hour consecutive limit • No overnight supervision • Twice monthly average (3-month period) 4\. Document in child's record | Initial approval and annually | Radius | TAC §749.2621 |
| Quality Assurance | 1\. Review babysitting usage quarterly 2\. Identify patterns exceeding limits 3\. Address overuse with case manager 4\. Document review findings | Quarterly | Radius Reports | T3C Blueprint FC 18.1 |

### 3\. INTERMITTENT ALTERNATIVE CARE (IAC)

#### Purpose and Overview

IAC represents our most comprehensive substitute care option, providing 72 hours to 14 days of care by fully verified foster families. As stated in policy, "IAC is the only form of substitute care permitted in response to an emergency or crisis," highlighting its critical role in maintaining placement stability during challenging times. The T3C funding model's inclusion of 40 days of paid IAC annually demonstrates the system's recognition of caregiver support needs.

| Who | How | When | Where | Regulatory Reference |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Caregiver | 1\. Submit IAC request including: • Reason for request • Preferred dates • Preferred IAC provider if known • Child's current needs/behaviors 2\. For tribal youth, note tribal affiliation | Minimum 2 weeks advance for planned IAC; immediately for emergencies | Radius or email to case manager | TAC §749.2633 |
| Case Manager | 1\. Review IAC request considering: • Child's service plan requirements • Previous IAC usage (10-day gap rule) • Annual limits (40 days) • Provider availability and suitability 2\. For tribal youth: • Contact tribal representative • Document approval/input • Follow ICWA requirements 3\. Match child to appropriate IAC provider 4\. Verify provider home compliance: • Current verification status • Capacity availability • 10-day gap since last IAC • Annual 60-day limit compliance | Within 48 hours of request | Radius IAC Module | TAC §749.2633; ICWA requirements |
| Program Director/CPMS | 1\. Review and approve IAC placement 2\. Consider: • Geographic proximity • Provider's ability to meet needs • Capacity ratios • Special circumstances 3\. Document approval rationale 4\. \*\*For investigations: Determine payment continuation per DFPS guidance\*\* | Within 1 business day of case manager review | Radius | TAC §749.2635 |
| Case Manager | 1\. Prepare IAC Fact Sheet including: • Child's routine and preferences • Medical information and medications • Educational requirements • Behavioral strategies using TBRI • Emergency contacts • Court-ordered visits • Cultural considerations 2\. Coordinate handoff between families 3\. Ensure medication transfer with logs 4\. Document placement in Radius | 48 hours before IAC start | Radius and direct communication | TAC §749.1307 |
| IAC Provider | 1\. Review child's information thoroughly 2\. Maintain all routines and appointments 3\. Implement TBRI strategies consistently 4\. Document daily activities and behaviors 5\. Administer medications per directions 6\. Maintain communication with: • Primary caregiver • Case manager • On-call support 7\. Complete daily logs | Throughout IAC placement | IAC provider home | TAC §749.2961 |
| Finance Manager | 1\. Process IAC payments per policy: • Regular family continues receiving rate • IAC provider receives same rate • Both paid on 15th and last day 2\. Track against 40-day annual funding 3\. \*\*For investigations over 14 days:\*\* • Suspend regular family payment unless DFPS approves • Document DFPS determination • Continue IAC provider payment | Per semi-monthly schedule | Radius Financial Module | T3C Blueprint p.404; RCC Contract Financial Terms |

### 4\. IN-HOME SUBSTITUTE CARE

#### Purpose and Overview

In-home substitute care allows children to remain in their familiar environment while their regular caregiver is away for more than 8 hours but less than 14 days. This option minimizes disruption for children who may struggle with transitions while ensuring qualified supervision.

| Who | How | When | Where | Regulatory Reference |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Caregiver | 1\. Request in-home care: • 3 weeks advance notice • Identify proposed provider • Specify dates and duration | Minimum 3 weeks before needed | Written notice to Refuge House | TAC §749.2635 |
| Case Manager/Home Development/Intake | 1\. Verify provider meets requirements: • 21+ years old • All background checks clear • Required training complete • Able to meet child's needs 2\. Complete justification form | Within 1 week of request | Radius | Provider standards |
| Case Manager | 1\. Prepare provider with: • Home orientation • Child routines • Emergency procedures • Medication protocols • TBRI strategies 2\. Document preparation | Before care begins | In-person at foster home | TAC §749.1307 |

### 5\. SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES

#### Purpose and Overview

Certain situations require modifications to standard substitute care procedures while maintaining safety and regulatory compliance. These circumstances include extended investigations, hospitalizations, and emergency placements.

| Who | How | When | Where | Regulatory Reference |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Program Director | **For Investigations:** 1\. Coordinate with DFPS on placement 2\. Determine payment structure: • IAC provider always paid • Regular family payment per DFPS 3\. Document all decisions 4\. Extend IAC beyond 14 days if needed 5\. Complete new paperwork every 14 days | Upon investigation notice | Radius and DFPS communication | TAC §749.2633 |
| Case Manager | **For Hospitalizations:** 1\. Arrange appropriate supervision 2\. Consider medical needs 3\. Coordinate with medical team 4\. Document all arrangements 5\. Update Safety Plan if needed | Immediately upon admission | Hospital and Radius | Medical consent requirements |
| On-Call Supervisor | **For Emergency Placements:** 1\. Identify available IAC provider 2\. Gather essential information 3\. Approve emergency placement 4\. Notify case manager 5\. Complete documentation within 24 hours | Immediately upon emergency | Phone and Radius | Emergency protocols |

### 6\. QUALITY ASSURANCE AND COMPLIANCE

#### Purpose and Overview

Regular monitoring ensures all substitute care arrangements meet regulatory requirements while supporting positive outcomes for children and families. Quality assurance activities identify trends, address concerns proactively, and maintain program integrity.

| Who | How | When | Where | Regulatory Reference |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Quality Assurance | 1\. Monitor compliance with: • Babysitting frequency limits • IAC day gaps and annual limits • Provider verification status • Documentation completeness • Payment accuracy 2\. Generate compliance reports 3\. Address issues with supervisors | Monthly and quarterly reviews | Radius Reports | T3C Blueprint monitoring |
| Treatment Director | 1\. Review substitute care usage patterns 2\. Identify support needs 3\. Recommend additional resources 4\. Ensure TBRI implementation 5\. Document reviews | Quarterly | Case reviews | CQI requirements |
| Finance Manager | 1\. Reconcile all substitute care payments 2\. Track against T3C allocations 3\. Identify billing discrepancies 4\. Report to Program Director 5\. Maintain audit trail | Monthly | Financial reports | Financial compliance |
