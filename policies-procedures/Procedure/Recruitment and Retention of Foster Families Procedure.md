## PURPOSE:

To establish comprehensive implementation procedures for recruiting, developing, and retaining trauma-informed foster families capable of providing T3C Basic Foster Family Home Support Services and applicable Add-On Services, ensuring seamless integration between recruitment activities and T3C credentialing requirements while maintaining robust support systems for long-term family retention.

## RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN POLICY AND PROCEDURE:

This procedure document implements the governing principles established in FC-16 Recruitment and Retention of Foster Families Policy, which commits Refuge House to recruiting and retaining diverse foster families capable of providing trauma-informed care through TBRI® principles. This procedure provides specific step-by-step instructions for how staff operationalize recruitment activities, family development processes, and retention support services while integrating T3C credentialing requirements as detailed in FC-T3C-01.1.

### 1\. RECRUITMENT ACTIVITIES AND COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT

#### Purpose and Overview

Effective recruitment requires a multi-faceted approach that reaches diverse communities while clearly communicating the specialized nature of T3C service requirements. Our recruitment efforts focus on identifying families who have the capacity to provide trauma-informed care and who can successfully complete T3C credentialing requirements. Building on our policy commitment to recruit diverse foster families, these procedures ensure that prospective families understand both the challenges and rewards of providing T3C-level care.

### 2\. INITIAL CONTACT AND FAMILY ENGAGEMENT

#### Purpose and Overview

The initial contact phase sets the foundation for successful foster family development and eventual T3C credentialing. During this critical period, families receive comprehensive information about both standard foster care requirements and the enhanced expectations of T3C service delivery. This phase leverages our existing network of current families, emancipated youth, personnel, and stakeholders to provide personal connections and authentic insight into the fostering experience.

| Who | How | When | Where | Regulatory Reference |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Home Development Department | 1\. Respond to prospective foster parent inquiries by phone and e-mail 2\. \*\*Provide comprehensive information about T3C service requirements and TBRI® training expectations\*\* 3\. Forward all initial paperwork via e-mail immediately following conversation 4\. \*\*Include T3C credentialing overview in initial information packet\*\*5. Create and begin prospective foster family contact log for tracking | Within same business day of initial contact | Phone or email with follow-up information packet | FC-16.01 |
| Home Development Department | 1\. Schedule and conduct initial home screening visit within 10 days of cleared criminalsrials 2\. \*\*Assess family's readiness for T3C credentialing requirements during initial visit\*\* 3\. ### 4\. HOME STUDY COORDINATION AND T3C ASSESSMENT INTEGRATION

#### Purpose and Overview

The home study process serves dual purposes: meeting DFPS licensing requirements and conducting the initial assessment for T3C credentialing capability. This integrated approach ensures families are evaluated comprehensively for their ability to provide both standard foster care and specialized T3C services using trauma-informed approaches.

**CRITICAL REQUIREMENT:** All Home Studies must be completed prior to a family being officially verified and able to receive placements per TAC §749.1301.
