    - **§749.111** - Placement Policies (determine appropriateness, match child with home, place siblings together, maintain sibling contact)
    - **§749.113** - Child-Care Policies (18 required policy areas including visitation, mail, telephone, gifts, clothing, possessions, EBI, religious programs, education, trips, program expectations, grievance procedures, parent reports, medical/dental, pregnancy/childbirth, primary medical needs, transitional living, weapons)
    - **§749.115** - Emergency Behavior Intervention Policies (complete description, specific techniques, qualifications, requirements/restrictions, orientation, training, prohibitions on retaliation)
    - **§749.117** - Discipline Policies (guide caregivers/employees, trauma informed care, positive responses, nurturing behavior, no discipline for infants)
    - **§749.119** - Foster Care Policies (screening criteria, placement decisions, training requirements, multiple care types)
    - **§749.121** - Rights and Responsibilities Policies (12 required areas including decision-making, training, communication, reimbursement, information sharing, support services, reporting, service planning, appeals, compliance, record review)
    - **§749.123** - Policies for Primary Medical Needs (overnight care 72 hours/year, respite care)
    - **§749.125** - Transitional Living Program Policies (5 required areas)
    - **§749.127** - Babysitter/Overnight/Respite Provider Policies (10 required areas including age, experience, training, references, number of children, information sharing, care instructions, contact method, approval, documentation)
    - **§749.129** - Legal Risk Placement Program Policies (requirements for families, criteria for selecting children)
    - **§749.131** - Adoption Policies (8 required areas including procedures/criteria, training, rights/responsibilities, cultural identity, fees, birth parent services, post adoption services)
    - **§749.133** - Volunteer Policies (5 required areas: job descriptions, qualifications/screening/selection, orientation/training, supervision, contact with children)
    - **§749.135** - Abuse and Neglect Policies (5 required areas: annual training, awareness methods, prevention techniques, coordination strategies, parent actions)
    - **§749.136** - Suicide Prevention, Intervention, and Postvention Policy (model policy or approved alternative, includes prevention training, screening, intervention, postvention)
    - **§749.137** - Model Suicide Prevention Policy (purpose, definitions, prevention training, screening, intervention, postvention)
    - **§749.138** - General Requirements for Policies (awareness and compliance)
    - **§749.139** - Requirements for Adopting Plans/Policies/Procedures (date of adoption, effective date, formalization by ownership type)
    - **§749.141** - Can Licensing Cite for Policy Violations (yes, if violates required policies)
  - **Division 2: Operational Responsibilities and Notifications (§749.151-159)**
    - **§749.151** - Operational Responsibilities (8 requirements including administrator, operate per plans/policies, maintain records, allow inspections, no conflicting services, liability insurance, budget control)
    - **§749.153** - Changes to Notify Licensing (30 days: legal structure, relocation, branch office, hours; 15 days: plans/policies; 2 days: administrator change, controlling person changes; foster home verification status)
    - **§749.154** - Timeframe for Filling Administrator Position (60 days if no back-up)
    - **§749.155** - Liability Insurance Requirements ($300,000 per occurrence, proof annually)
    - **§749.157** - Acceptable Reasons Not to Have Insurance (financial reasons, no underwriter, exhausted limits)
    - **§749.159** - Notify Parents About No Insurance (before admission, within 14 days if stop carrying)
  - **Division 3: General Fiscal Requirements (§749.161)**
    - **§749.161** - Fiscal Requirements (sound fiscal basis, pay employees/foster parents timely, meet children's needs, maintain financial records, account for child's money separately, annual review or reserve funds)
  - **Division 4: Fiscal Requirements for Adoption Agencies (§749.191-199)**
    - **§749.191** - Annual Financial Report for Adoption Services (income from all sources, expenses by category, submit within 60 days of fiscal year end)
    - **§749.193** - Payments for Adoption Referrals Prohibited
    - **§749.195** - Fees Prior to Home Study Completion (application fees, home study fees, education/training fees)
    - **§749.197** - Adoption Fee Policies (fee or fee schedule, pass-through expenses, additional fees explanation, refund policy)
    - **§749.199** - Must Charge Same Fees for All Adoptions (no, may consider hard-to-place, sliding scale, parameters must be specified and equally applied)
  - **Division 5: Financial Assistance to Birth Mothers (§749.231-245)**
    - **§749.231** - Financial Assistance for Birth Mother (reasonable and necessary living expenses and legal costs, 6 included expenses, 4 excluded expenses)
    - **§749.233** - Time Period for Financial Assistance (during pregnancy, postpartum care)
    - **§749.235** - Determine Birth Mother's Need (review financial resources, evaluate family support/insurance/other resources, justify payment, document evaluation)
    - **§749.237** - Document Financial Assistance (receipts with date, payee, purpose, maintain in birth mother's record)
    - **§749.239** - Cash Payments to Birth Mothers (allowed for day-to-day purchases, policies required, maximum per category, documentation, up to one month)
    - **§749.241** - Cannot Require Repayment (inform birth mother in writing, post in offices, in birth mother's language)
    - **§749.243** - Foster Care Services Free/Reduced Rate (allowed if not contingent on relinquishment)
    - **§749.245** - Cannot Disrupt Existing Resources (unless best interest determination based on documented health/safety impact)
  - **Division 6: Fiscal Accountability/Pass-Through Expenses (§749.271-273)**
    - **§749.271** - May Require Reimbursement for Birth Mother Expenses (may pass through certain expenses, cannot pass through if met by insurance/other source)
    - **§749.273** - Requirements for Pass-Through Expenses (8 requirements including fee policy description, individual report, itemized list, written estimate, agreement if exceed 10%, refund surplus, inform about no repayment)
  - **Division 7: Branch Offices (§749.301-307)**
    - **§749.301** - What Is Branch Office (where child placement staff and master records located, may operate if compliant and in good standing)
    - **§749.303** - Before Opening Branch Office (30 days prior, 5 required items: address/phone/hours, administrative staff, child placement management staff, other employees, updated professional staffing plan)
    - **§749.304** - Where Must Have Main/Branch Office (in each region where verify homes, or within 150 miles of each verified foster home)
    - **§749.305** - Requirements for Administrators/Treatment Directors (separate for each office, or shared if caseload limits met: 35 child-care/25 treatment/30 combination children, 15 homes, 30 combination cases)
    - **§749.307** - When Branch Office Closes (if corrective/adverse action: close or transfer homes with updated study; if not: may transfer without updating)
  - **Division 9: Clients and Appeals (§749.421-425)**
    - **§749.421** - Who Are Clients (child clients: foster care, pre-consummated adoptive placement; adult clients: 7 types including birth parents, foster/adoptive applicants/parents, young adults)
    - **§749.423** - Rights of Adult Clients (4 rights: review rules/compliance reports/policies, right to appeal, complaint procedures, other entities for complaints)
    - **§749.425** - Appeal Process Requirements (written process, 6 required elements, provide before making person client, internal review sufficient)

## Key Requirements Relevant to Refuge House

### Service Planning (§749.1301-1335)
- **30-Day Service Plan Timeline** (§749.1301) - CRITICAL HIGH WEIGHT STANDARD
  - Service plans must be completed within 30 days of placement
  - Referenced in: FC-T3C-01, FC3-01, FC2-01, FC3-01.1
  - All policies emphasize compliance with this timeline

### Admission Assessment (§749.1301-1317)
- **Assessment Timelines:**
  - Emergency Placement: Assessment initiated within 24 hours
  - Routine Placement: Assessment follows standard timelines
- **Required Components:**
  - Trauma history and previous experiences
  - Attachment patterns and relationships
  - Behavioral incidents in past 30 days
  - Medical/mental health diagnoses
  - Educational placement and needs
  - Cultural/religious background
- Referenced in: FC2-01 (Admission Assessment Policy), FC2-01.1 (Admission Assessment Procedure)

### Medical and Dental Care (§749.1401-1435)
- Comprehensive healthcare coordination requirements
- STAR Health coordination protocols
- Referenced in: FC10-01, FC10-01.1, FC10-01.2

### Crisis Management (§749.339, §749.901)
- Emergency behavior intervention requirements
- Personal restraint protocols
- SAMA training requirements
- Referenced in: FC-04 (Crisis Management Policy and Procedure)
